Steps to create Veg-Biryani:-

  1. Rinse 1 ½ cups of Basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare your mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, green beans, potatoes, bell peppers) by chopping them into small pieces.
  3. In a dry pan, roast the following spices until fragrant:1 tablespoon coriander seeds,1 teaspoon cumin seeds,2-3 cloves1 small cinnamon stick,1-2 dry red chilies (adjust to taste)Let them cool and then grind to a fine powder and set aside.
  4. In a large pot, bring 6 cups of water to a boil. Add salt, 1-2 green cardamoms, a small cinnamon stick, 2-3 cloves, and 1 bay leaf.
  5. Add the soaked and drained rice to the boiling water. Cook until the rice is about 70% cooked (it should still have a bite). Drain the rice and set aside.
  6. Heat Oil : In a large, heavy-bottomed pot (or pressure cooker), heat 2 tablespoons of ghee or oil over medium heat.
    Add Whole Spices: Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Then add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
    Add Green Chilies and Ginger-Garlic Paste: Stir in the slit green chilies and ginger-garlic paste, and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
    Add Chopped Tomatoes: Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
    Add Mixed Vegetables: Stir in the chopped mixed vegetables and cook for 2-3 minutes.
    Add Spice Powders: Add the roasted biryani masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well to coat the vegetables with the spices. Cook for another 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Layer Rice: Once the vegetables are cooked, spread half of the cooked rice over the vegetable mixture.
    Add Herbs and Saffron: Sprinkle half of the chopped coriander and mint leaves over the rice, along with fried onions, saffron soaked in warm milk, and a tablespoon of melted ghee.
    Add Remaining Rice: Layer the remaining rice on top and repeat the process with the remaining herbs, saffron, and ghee.
  8. Seal the Pot: Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. You can also seal the edges with a dough made of flour to trap the steam.
    Cook on Low Heat: Cook on low heat (dum) for about 25-30 minutes. If using a pressure cooker, you can place a heavy pot on top of the lid for even heat distribution, but do not use the pressure.
    Check for Doneness: After 25-30 minutes, turn off the heat and let it sit for another 10 minutes before opening.
  9. Fluff the Biryani: Carefully fluff the biryani with a fork to mix the layers.
    Garnish: Serve hot, garnished with additional coriander leaves and fried onions. You can also serve it with raita (yogurt sauce) or salad.

Steps to create Chana Masala:-

  1. Soak 1 cup of dried chickpeas in plenty of water for at least 8 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain and rinse the soaked chickpeas.In a pressure cooker, add the soaked chickpeas and 3 cups of water.Cook on high heat for 2-3 whistles, then lower the heat and cook for another 10-15 minutes until soft. If using canned chickpeas, skip this step and just rinse and drain them.
  3. In a large pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil or ghee over medium heat.
  4. Add 1 finely chopped onion and sauté until it becomes golden brown.
  5. Stir in 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  6. Add 2 chopped or pureed tomatoes and cook until they are soft and oil starts to separate from the mixture.
  7. Add the cooked chickpeas to the masala mixture. If using canned chickpeas, add them at this stage.
  8. Stir in 1 teaspoon of garam masala and 1 teaspoon of chana masala powder (if using). Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice, jeera rice, or Indian bread like naan or chapati. Enjoy your delicious Chana Masala!

Steps to create Rajma Chawla

  1. Rinse the kidney beans and soak them in enough water overnight (or for at least 8 hours). Drain and set aside.
  2. In a pressure cooker, add the soaked rajma and 3 cups of fresh water.Pressure cook for about 15-20 minutes or until the beans are soft and cooked through. If using a regular pot, it may take longer (about 1.5 to 2 hours).
  3. Mix in salt, red chili powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder, and coriander powder. Cook for about 5 minutes until the oil separates from the masala.
  4. Once the rajma is cooked, add it to the masala mixture. Stir well to combine.If the mixture is too thick, add a little water to reach your desired consistency.Let it simmer for 10-15 minutes on low heat. Stir occasionally and adjust salt/spice as needed.Add garam masala towards the end and mix well.
  5. Serve the hot rajma in a bowl alongside a portion of steaming basmati rice (chawal).You can garnish the rajma with fresh coriander leaves if desired.

Steps to create Bisi bele bath

  1. Rinse and soak ½ cup of rice and ¼ cup of toor dal (split pigeon peas) in water for about 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Chop the vegetables (carrot, green beans, potato, green peas, and tomato) into bite-sized pieces.
  3. Allow the roasted spices to cool, then grind them into a fine powder. Set aside.
  4. Temper the Spices: In a large pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil or ghee.1 teaspoon mustard seeds,10-12 curry leaves,1-2 dry red chilies,A pinch of asafoetida (hing).
    Sauté Onions: Add 1 large chopped onion and sauté until golden brown.
    Add Ginger-Garlic: Stir in 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
    Add Tomatoes: Add 2 medium chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy.
  5. Add the Ground Masala: Mix in the ground Bisi Bele Bath masala and sauté for a minute until fragrant.
    Combine Rice and Dal Mixture: Add the cooked rice, dal, and vegetable mixture to the pan. Mix everything gently to combine.
    Adjust Consistency: Add water if needed to adjust the consistency. Stir well and let it simmer on low heat for about 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
    Final Seasoning: Add salt to taste and a little jaggery (if using) to balance flavors.
  6. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.

Steps to create Panner Butter Masala

  1. If you prefer, lightly fry the paneer cubes in a little oil or butter until they are golden brown. This step adds flavor and a nice texture to the paneer. Remove and set aside.
  2. Heat the Oil and Butter: In a pan or kadai, heat 2 tablespoons of butter and 1 tablespoon of oil over medium heat. The oil helps prevent the butter from burning.
    Add Onions: Once the butter melts, add the finely chopped onions. Sauté them until they turn golden brown.
    Add Ginger-Garlic Paste: Add 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and sauté for about a minute until the raw smell disappears.
    Add Green Chilies: If you want extra heat, add slit green chilies to the mixture and sauté for another minute.
  3. Pour in the pureed tomatoes. Cook until the mixture thickens and the oil starts to separate from the gravy (about 5-7 minutes) and add spices.
  4. Add Paneer: Gently add the fried or raw paneer cubes into the gravy, mixing carefully to coat them well with the sauce.
    Add Cream: Pour in the fresh cream (or cashew cream) and stir well to combine. Simmer for about 5 minutes on low heat to allow the flavors to meld.
  5. Stir in 1 teaspoon of garam masala and 1 teaspoon of crushed kasuri methi. Mix well and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  6. Turn off the heat and garnish the dish with fresh coriander leaves.

Steps to create Butter Chicken:-

  1. Mix Ingredients: In a bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, cumin powder, salt, and lemon juice. Mix well to form a marinade.
    Add Chicken: Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Cover and let it marinate for at least 1 hour (or overnight in the refrigerator for best results).
  2. Heat Oil: In a large pan or skillet, heat a tablespoon of oil over medium heat.
    Cook Chicken: Add the marinated chicken (discard excess marinade) and cook until it's browned and cooked through, about 6-8 minutes. Remove the chicken from the pan and set aside.
  3. Sauté Onions: In the same pan, add butter. Once melted, add finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
    Add Ginger-Garlic Paste: Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute until the raw smell disappears.
    Add Tomato Puree: Add the pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil starts to separate from the mixture, about 8-10 minutes.
    Add Spices: Stir in red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for another minute.
    Finish the Sauce: Add garam masala and kasuri methi. Stir to combine.
  4. Add Chicken Back: Return the cooked chicken to the pan. Mix well to coat the chicken in the sauce.
    Add Cream: Pour in the fresh cream and stir to combine. Cook for another 5 minutes on low heat. If the sauce is too thick, you can add a little water to reach your desired consistency.
    Taste and Adjust: Taste the sauce and adjust salt, sugar, or spices as needed.
  5. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and additional cream if desired.

Steps to create Prawn Thokku:-

  1. In a bowl, mix the cleaned prawns with salt, turmeric powder, and a pinch of red chili powder. Let them marinate for about 15-20 minutes.
  2. In a small pan, dry roast the coriander seeds, cumin seeds, and black pepper (if using) until fragrant. Let them cool, then grind them into a fine powder. Set aside.
  3. In a heavy-bottomed pan or kadai, heat the oil over medium heat. Coconut oil adds authentic flavor, but any cooking oil will work.
  4. Once the oil is hot, add the mustard seeds. When they start to splutter, add the curry leaves and a pinch of asafoetida (if using).
  5. Add the finely chopped onions and green chilies to the pan. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
  6. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  7. Add the chopped or pureed tomatoes to the pan. Cook until the tomatoes break down and the oil starts to separate from the mixture, about 5-7 minutes.
  8. Add salt, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and the freshly ground spice mix. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  9. Add the marinated prawns to the pan. Stir to coat the prawns well with the masala. Cook for about 5-7 minutes or until the prawns are cooked through (they will turn pink).
  10. Stir in the garam masala and mix well. If you prefer a tangy flavor, add tamarind paste at this stage. Cook for another minute.
  11. Turn off the heat and garnish the dish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.

Steps to create Lamb Chops:-

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a bowl, mix together the olive oil, minced garlic, chopped rosemary, thyme, mint (if using), salt, pepper, and lemon juice.
    Marinate the Lamb: Place the lamb chops in a shallow dish or a resealable plastic bag. Pour the marinade over the chops, ensuring they are well coated.
  2. Cover the dish or seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 1 hour. For best results, marinate for 4-6 hours or overnight to enhance the flavor.
  3. Before cooking, take the lamb chops out of the refrigerator and let them sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes. This helps in even cooking.
  4. Preheat the Grill or Pan:
    If grilling, preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
    If pan-searing, heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and add a little olive oil.
    Cook the Lamb Chops:
    Grilling: Place the marinated lamb chops on the grill. Cook for about 4-5 minutes on each side for medium-rare, adjusting the time according to your preferred doneness.
    Pan-searing: Place the lamb chops in the hot skillet. Cook for about 4-5 minutes on each side for medium-rare.
  5. Once cooked to your liking, transfer the lamb chops to a plate. Cover them loosely with aluminum foil and let them rest for about 5-10 minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute.

Steps to create Murgh Dum Biryani

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a large bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, salt, lemon juice, chopped mint, and coriander leaves.
    Add Chicken: Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and mix well until the chicken is fully coated.
    Marinate: Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight for better flavor.
  2. Rinse and Soak: Rinse the soaked basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This removes excess starch.
    Boil Water: In a large pot, bring 6 cups of water to a boil. Add green cardamoms, cinnamon stick, cloves, bay leaf, and salt.
    Cook Rice: Add the soaked rice to the boiling water. Cook until the rice is about 70% done (it should still have a bite). Drain the rice and set aside.
  3. Cook Chicken: In a heavy-bottomed pot or pressure cooker, heat ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the marinated chicken and cook until the chicken is browned and cooked through (about 10-15 minutes).
    Layering Ingredients: Once the chicken is cooked, remove half of the chicken from the pot and set aside.
  4. Layer Rice: Spread half of the cooked rice evenly over the chicken in the pot.
    Add Garnishes: Sprinkle half of the fried onions (birista), fresh mint, and coriander leaves over the rice. Drizzle with melted ghee and saffron milk.
    Add Remaining Chicken: Add the remaining chicken over the rice layer and then top with the remaining rice.
    Final Garnish: Finish with the rest of the fried onions, mint, coriander leaves, and a drizzle of melted ghee.
  5. Seal the Pot: Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. To prevent steam from escaping, you can seal the edges with dough or use a kitchen towel before placing the lid.
    Cook on Dum: Reduce the heat to low and cook for about 25-30 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld and the chicken to become tender.
    Check Occasionally: After 25 minutes, check the biryani. The rice should be fluffy, and the chicken should be cooked through.
  6. Gently fluff the biryani with a fork to mix the layers without breaking the rice.

Steps to create Spicy Chettinad Chicken

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a bowl, combine the chicken pieces with yogurt (if using), turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well to coat the chicken evenly.
    Marinate: Allow the chicken to marinate for at least 30 minutes. For best results, marinate it for a few hours or overnight in the refrigerator.
  2. Toast the Spices: In a small pan, heat 2 teaspoons of oil over low heat. Add the coriander seeds, cumin seeds, fennel seeds, black peppercorns, poppy seeds (if using), dry red chilies, cloves, cinnamon stick, green cardamoms, star anise (if using), curry leaves, and cashews or coconut pieces.
    Roast: Stir and roast the spices for 2-3 minutes until they become fragrant. Be careful not to burn them.
    Grind the Masala: Allow the roasted spices to cool slightly, then grind them into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle. Set aside
  3. Heat Oil: In a large pot or heavy pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil over medium heat.
    Sauté Onions: Add the finely chopped onion and sauté until golden brown.
    Add Green Chilies and Ginger-Garlic Paste: Add the slit green chilies and ginger-garlic paste. Sauté for another 2-3 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
    Add Tomatoes: Stir in the chopped tomatoes and salt. Cook until the tomatoes soften and oil begins to separate from the mixture.
  4. Add the Marinated Chicken: Add the marinated chicken to the pan, mixing well to coat it with the onion-tomato mixture.
    Add the Chettinad Masala: Sprinkle the ground Chettinad masala over the chicken and mix well.
    Simmer: Cover the pan and cook on low heat for about 25-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. If needed, add a little water to prevent the chicken from sticking.
  5. Serve the Spicy Chettinad Chicken hot with steamed rice, naan, or parathas.
